Work Sample: Chronoshift Expeditions.

My First Website


The sample presented is my very first website I ever developed as a practice project, known as Chronoshift Expeditions. To me, this project is considered as the very first mainframe of my web development and programming pathway, which also piqued my interest further into computers. This project first started off with thorough planning and a structural design process after reading the short story, “A Sound of Thunder”, by Ray Bradbury. Creating various contents, containing subjects such as: a home page, an about page, an expeditions page, a rules page, and a booking page, were essential requirements to arrange the key information and purpose of the website.

On the other hand, after conducting planning and explanatory detail for the website, I was tasked with creating a wireframe that would serve as a template for the website. The wireframe itself would be of service for when I developed the coding in visual studio code. Using visual studio code, I used HTML5 and CSS3 to design the website basing it off the wireframe I created. As part of the technical aspect as well, I had to ensure my HTML was thoroughly corrected through an HTML validator, making sure my code was valid. However, if I had to say, my biggest challenge on this project was developing media queries. Media queries were extremely crucial, as content on the website needed specific spacing, text alignment, or even content placement. I struggled directly with using flexboxes as I didn’t really have a good grasp of it. I resolved this by teaching myself and informing myself on media queries using youtube lessons as a reference. Additionally, I referred back to media query notes I had written. Finally as a result, I was able to finalize my project. As part of my self-reflection, if I had something to take away from this project, it would be that trying your best and even with a new subject, topic, or what it may be, continue to persist and don’t be afraid to explore or play around with your own creativity. Nonetheless, as one of the CART “Habits of Mind” included, I showcased the investigate, persist, and reflect habits of mind. Lastly, without the support, feedback, and positive criticism from my peers, I would’ve definitely had a more difficult time creating my first website.
