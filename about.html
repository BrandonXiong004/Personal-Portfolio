Who Am I?

My name is Brandon Xiong, and I am currently nearing the end of the path to graduate from Floyd B Buchanan High School, class of 2022, and will be moving on to the next chapter in my life. Concurrently as of now, I am also attending the Center of Advanced Research and Technology(CART) as part of the web development and application lab, whereas last year from 2020-21, I attended the cybersecurity lab. I am insatiably always wanting to learn more about computers and I want to be able to take my time to understand the framework and boundaries of where computers can lead us in the future!
As a student in the web application development lab, I utilize various applications used to design websites such as Figma, Lucidchart, and Visual Studio Code. In more detail, Figma is a vector graphics editor used to design layouts, shapes, text, and coordination of applications, as well as websites themselves. On the other hand, Lucidchart is used to design flowcharts, either proposed for the use of algorithms, decisions, and network connections. Moreover, Visual Studio Code is a source-code editor, mainly used with the intent of developing programs, softwares, and coding information together to either exchange, create, restore, or delete data. Cybersecurity on the other hand, I explored the malicious attack programs developed by people with the intent of using it for unethical purposes. Additionally, I learned how with further experience, we can mostly prevent these attacks from happening by understanding the language of how these malicious programs work, and design an encryption algorithm which counter these attacks.

To illustrate more about my professional work, in the web development lab I have orchestrated and layed out wireframes essential to designing websites made by me. In the lab, there is lots of collaboration between peers working together from time to time usually for assistance to help accomplish individual goals and work. But when it comes to conducting actual designs and websites as a team, I have done my share of always trying my best to accomplish my work and always trying to look out for my team members and accommodating them. As for my cybersecurity lab, I have conducted research based problems along with a mentor in order to derive a solution. These research based problems often accounted for connections of professional work relations in the computer field, computer networking, hardware manufacturing, and computer information systems.

On the matter, to describe myself as a person, I have a deep interest in computers such as PC designs, development and code, and cars. I believe that computers are revolutionary and highly innovative. While indeed they are still volatile as they can be, I believe that with the right purpose and intent they can help solve big complex problems that would take decades, if not centuries.
