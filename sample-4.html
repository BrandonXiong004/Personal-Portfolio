Work Sample: Interactive Story.

Latest Project


The sample presented is an interactive story project I created working with a partner. For this particular project, each partner had to work together collectively to create an interactive story based on their own theme, story genre, and creativity. For my project, my partner and I decided on a fictional fantasy story. The story takes place with the main character living in modern-day time, who is transported to another world where the era of swords and magic exists.

In emphasis, the project first began with an outline of requirements and criteria we had to follow. First and foremost, a written project description had to be drafted before any flowcharting and programming could be done. After a final draft of the description, my partner and I began creating a flowchart, mapping out each decision and what output it branches out to. Prior to creating the flowchart, a program logic needed to be programmed in order to give ourselves a base structure of an algorithm that updates the screen or progresses the story. On the daily, personal journal entries had to be completed, task lists had to be updated, and a meeting with our instructor had to be met on a specific deadline to stay updated with our work.

On the other hand, after the program flowchart was completed, my partner and I began the coding on visual studio code. In visual studio code, we mainly used HTML5 to format the website layout, CSS3 for the visuals such as decoration for the website and background, and lastly JavaScript for our story progression. Additionally, JavaScript was probably the most challenging part of this project. This was because a particular array that held our story content was not being iterated within our story. This was a big problem because, without the contents of the story, the text for the story would not be updated or refreshed in the story section. However, a solution to fix this was by reconfiguring the different indexes of the array and editing the code. An example of this would be using: [array].option, rather than [array].options. The sample letter of an ‘s’ caused us rather inconvenient problems, but once figured out, the story worked just fine. If I had to state what CART Habits of Mind I displayed, it would be the investigate, collaborate, and persist habits of mind. Investigate, because careful analysis of my code had to be observed to resolve errors. Collaboration with my partner was really important and it was a big help to be able to have a partner as well. Lastly, persistence was demonstrated as I stayed motivated to complete the story.
